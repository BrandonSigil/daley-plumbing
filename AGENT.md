## 2. Brand Voice & Tone

### The vibe

Think: a local brewery, not a home brewer. A founding philosophy baked into a real company — not a one-man band. The values and decade of experience came from one person, but the brand belongs to the _company_. Wes Anderson aesthetic: precise, warm, a little quirky, never loud. Vintage-leaning, homespun but not amateur.

### Three brand pillars — every piece of content should hit at least one:

- **Trust** — Licensed, insured, a decade of doing it right. No mystery charges, no runaround.
- **Expertise** — Specializes in new construction. Knows the code, knows the tradespeople, gets it done.
- **Warmth** — Local company. Knows the neighborhood. Shows up when they say they will. Treats your home like it has their name on it. Because it does.

### Tone descriptors

- Direct and no-nonsense, but warm
- Dry wit — never slapstick, never corporate
- Specific and local ("Pierce County," "South Sound," "Tacoma & the South Sound")
- Modest confidence: "we've seen this before" not "we're the best in the state"
- Written by a human, not a marketing department

### Positioning language

- We ARE: local, independent, licensed, experienced, community-rooted, growing
- We are NOT: a chain, a franchise, a side hustle, a one-man band, a national brand
- The phrase "not a chain" is a power move — use it to contrast against Roto-Rooter etc.

### Writing rules — always follow these

- Use "we" and "our" not "I" or "my" — the brand is the company, not the individual
- "Daley Plumbing" as an entity, not "I've been doing this for X years"
- ✅ "Daley Plumbing has been at this for over a decade"
- ❌ "I've been doing this for ten years"
- "10 years in the pipes" is a great line — attach it to the company, not a person
- Never: corporate-speak ("solutions," "leverage," "synergy")
- Never: desperation language ("BEST PRICES!!!" "CALL NOW!!!")
- Never: vague superlatives ("world-class service," "second to none")
- Never: tradesman clichés ("no job too small," "family owned and operated" without personality)
- Do use: dry humor, local specificity, confident understatement

### Approved brand phrases (use throughout the site)

- "Plumber for the People" — the tagline, use frequently
- "Serving Tacoma & the South Sound" — use on homepage, header, GBP
- "Built on 10 years of doing it right"
- "10 years in the pipes — and counting"
- "Tacoma-built. South Sound proud."
- "Local plumbers who actually care"
- "Independent. Local. Licensed."
- "The local alternative to the big guys"
- "Built right. From the ground up." (especially for new construction pages)
- "No mystery charges. No runaround."
- "Straight talk, fair price"

- "Work we stand behind"

- "A decade of South Sound plumbing"

### Phrases to retire / avoid

- ❌ "One-man operation"
- ❌ "Your neighbor with a pipe wrench"
- ❌ "Hi, I'm [Name]..." — don't open About page this way
- ❌ "I started this business because..." — use "Daley Plumbing started with..."

## 3. SEO Strategy

### Core philosophy

**Topical authority, not keyword stuffing.** Google penalizes stuffing. The winning strategy is one high-quality, genuinely useful page per service + one per city. Each page must answer what a real customer would search and include all the technical SEO elements below.

### Primary keywords to rank for

- "plumber Tacoma WA"
- "new construction plumber Tacoma"
- "plumber near me Tacoma"
- "new construction plumbing Pierce County"
- "plumber Federal Way WA"
- "plumber Puyallup WA"
- "plumber Gig Harbor WA"
- "emergency plumber Tacoma"
- "water heater replacement Tacoma"
- "drain cleaning Tacoma"
- "remodel plumber Tacoma"
- "licensed plumber Pierce County"

### Long-tail keyword targets (for blog and FAQ content)

- "how much does new construction plumbing cost in Tacoma"
- "what permits do I need for plumbing in Pierce County"
- "tankless vs tank water heater Pacific Northwest"
- "how to winterize pipes Tacoma"
- "what is rough-in plumbing new construction"
- "best time to hire a plumber for home remodel"
- "why is my water pressure low Federal Way"
- "how long does new construction plumbing take"
- "do I need a permit to replace a water heater in Washington state"

### On-page SEO rules — apply to every page

- **Title tag format:** `[Service] in [City] | Daley Plumbing | (224) 430-1420`
- **H1 format:** `[Service] in [City Name], WA` — always include geo in H1
- **Meta description:** 150–160 chars, include primary keyword + phone number or CTA
- **Phone number:** Clickable `tel:` link, visible above the fold on every page, especially mobile
- **Schema markup:** LocalBusiness + Plumber JSON-LD schema on every page
- **NAP consistency:** Name, Address, Phone must be identical everywhere — site, GBP, Yelp, Angi, etc.
- **Internal linking:** Every city page links to relevant service pages. Every service page links to city pages it serves.
- **Image alt text:** Always descriptive and include location/service where natural. Never leave blank.
- **Reviews:** Display Google reviews on homepage and service pages (embed or widget)
- **Mobile-first:** 60%+ of plumbing searches are on phones. Test everything on mobile first.
- **Page speed:** Compress all images. Target under 3 seconds load time.

### New Construction Plumbing (flagship service page)

**Title tag:** `New Construction Plumber in Tacoma, WA | Daley Plumbing`  
**Meta description:** `From rough-in to final trim, Daley Plumbing handles every phase of new construction plumbing in Pierce and King County. On time, on code, on budget. Call (224) 430-1420`  
**H1:** `New Construction Plumbing in the South Sound — Built Right from Day One`

**This page has two audiences: homeowners AND general contractors/builders. Address both.**

**Intro copy:**

> "From rough-in to final trim, Daley Plumbing handles every phase of new construction plumbing in Pierce and King County. We work with builders, contractors, and homeowners to keep projects on schedule and up to code — with the kind of communication that makes a job site actually run smoothly."

**Explain the phases (homeowners don't know these terms — define them briefly):**

- **Rough-in:** The first phase — running pipes through framing before walls close. Requires inspection.
- **Top-out (or above-slab):** Venting and drain lines roughed in above the slab or sub-floor.
- **Trim/finish:** Final phase — installing fixtures, supply lines, appliances after walls are done.

**Contractor-specific section:**

> "Working with a builder? We know how job sites work. We show up on time, communicate clearly with your GC and subs, and don't hold up your schedule. Ask about contractor rates for ongoing projects — one good relationship is worth more than a hundred one-off calls."

**Must include:**

- List of cities served for new construction (geo SEO)
- Specific mention of Pierce County permit knowledge
- Photo of new construction job site (real photo, not stock)
- "Work with us on your next build" CTA form — ask for project type, city, timeline, contact info
- FAQ section targeting long-tail keywords (cost, permits, timeline, what's included)

## 7. What NOT to Generate

When writing any copy for this site, never produce:

- Corporate filler ("At Daley Plumbing, we are committed to providing exceptional plumbing solutions...")
- Vague superlatives without specifics ("the best," "second to none," "world-class")
- Keyword stuffing — do not repeat a keyword more than 2–3 times per page naturally
- Stock-photo-style descriptions ("a smiling technician arrives at your door")
- Generic trust signals without substance ("customer satisfaction guaranteed" with no backup)
- First-person singular framing ("I do," "I offer," "I will") — use "we" throughout
- Anything that positions this as a solo side hustle or one-man operation
